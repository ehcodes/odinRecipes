Pasteles en Hoja

Description

Pasteles en hoja are a Dominican cultural favorite, often appearing at the table during Christmas and New Year's Eve. These tasty treats are a lot like tamales but instead of being made with corn, they are made with root vegetables.

Ingredients

Batter Ingredients

  • Blender or food processor
  • Your choice of filling
  • 1 plantain
  • 1/3 lb of taro
  • 1/3 lb of kabocha squash
  • 1 tablespoon seasoning powder
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoon salted butter
  • 1 tablespoon annato powder

Wrapping Ingredients

  • 12 Banana tree leaves squares
  • 12 pieces of parchment paper (cut to the same size as the banana leaves)
  • food safe twine

Filling Recipes

  • Beef
  • Chicken

Steps

  1. Chop the plantain, taro, and squash into chunks small enough for your blender or food processor to handle.
  2. Placed the chopped plantain, taro, and squash into your food processor or blender.
  3. Pureé the chopped vegetables until it's uniform, feeling as though it has grains of sand in it. This may take between 5-10 minutes. Make sure there are no clumps in the pureé.
  4. Place a piece of parchment paper on a clean level surface.
  5. Place a banana leaf directly on top of the parchment paper.
  6. Place 2 1/2 tbs of the batter in the center of the banana leaf.
  7. Place 2 tbs of your filling of choice on top of the batter that is already on the leaf.
  8. Place 2 more tbs of batter on top of your filling of choice.
  9. Fold the banana leaf into the rectangular shape of an envelope, encompassing your batter and filling.
  10. Fold the parchment paper into the rectangular shape of an envelope, around the banana leaf.
  11. Wrap the pasteles shut with your twine. For clearer instructions on how to wrap them, see this video.
  12. Repeat steps 4-11 until you've wrapped all 12 pasteles.
  13. Bring 1 gallon of water to a rolling boil (medium heat).
  14. Carefully place all 12 pasteles into the water.
  15. Place a lid on the pot and allow to boil for 20 minutes.
  16. Using tongs, flip the pasteles in the boiling water.
  17. Place a lid on the pot and allow to boil for another 20 minutes.
  18. It's time to test if they're ready! Carefully remove one of the pasteles from the water. Unwrap it and check if it's ready, it's consistency should resemble the photo above.
  19. If it's not ready, re-wrap the opened one and place it back into the pot. Boil the pasteles for another 10 minutes.
  20. Once the pasteles are ready, turn off the stove and carefully remove the water from the pot.
  21. Serve the pasteles immediately after unwrapping. They'll be piping hot and ready to enjoy, careful not to burn yourself!
